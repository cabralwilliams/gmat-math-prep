Arithmetic Series

Arithmetic Series are series that have a consistent gap between successive members of the series. This gap between the values in the series is known as the common difference. Another defining feature of any arithmetic series is that for a truncated series, the mean and median of the terms in question are always the same value. Note that a series in which all values are the same is an arithmetic series with common difference equal to zero.

Series Features

Here are the main features of an arithmetic series.
  • Common difference: same gap between successive members of the series
  • Identical mean and median
  • Specific term in series (d is the common difference)
    an = a1 + d⋅(n -1)
    an = a0 + d⋅n
  • Sum of truncated series of n terms
    Sn = 
    n⋅(a1 + an)
